New to Commuter Life?
Living as a commuter student is an opportunity for personal growth and development essential to life after college. We are excited to join you in this extraordinary stage of your college journey. We want to get to know you, support you, and make your college experience the best it can be, both on and off campus.

Living Off-Campus
Living off campus means learning how to navigate both Liberty and the city around it. From housing and transportation to food and weekly rhythms, the details matter. These resources are here to help you build a sustainable, steady life in Lynchburg as a commuter.

Living in Lynchburg
Liberty’s Off-Campus Housing site helps you search for apartments, find roommates, and see what’s available around Lynchburg.

The Lynchburg Community page connects you to neighborhoods, local businesses, and places you’ll start to recognize as part of your daily rhythm. 

Convocation as a Commuter
Convocation is part of Liberty’s weekly rhythm of worship, teaching, and campus life. As a commuter, you are invited—but not required—to attend. Convocation meets in the Vines Center on Wednesdays and Fridays at 10:30 a.m. Some commuters attend weekly, while others come when they can.
